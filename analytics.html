// Data & Insights

From Fitting Rooms to Data Models.

Service industry trained human insight meets academically-trained analysis.

01 — Economic Modeling

The Long Game

"For sustainable tech, the messaging must shift from 'price tag' to 'lifetime value'. The data proves the long-term win."

I modeled a 10-year cost comparison between Electric Vehicles (EV) and Internal Combustion Engines (ICE). While ICE cars are cheaper upfront, the EV demonstrates a clear financial advantage ($28k vs $36k) when factoring in fluctuating energy costs and maintenance. This insight anchors campaigns on "investment" rather than "cost."

Tools: Excel Forecasting, Discount Rate Analysis
02 — Behavioral Patterns

The City's Heartbeat

"Behavioral data is predictive. The 'prime time' for a utility campaign isn't TV prime time—it's the 4 PM summer spike."

Analyzing hourly energy load curves for NYC reveals the "human heartbeat" of the city. By isolating weekday vs. weekend variances and seasonal spikes, I identify precise windows for behavioral intervention campaigns. We don't guess when the audience is active; the grid tells us.

Tools: Time Series Analysis, Excel
03 — Psychographics

Beyond the Persona

"Higher education correlates with higher well-being, but health behaviors fracture along political lines. One message cannot fit all."

Analyzing a dataset of 1,000+ respondents revealed nuanced correlations between education, income, and well-being. The data dictated a segmented strategy: a "maintenance" message for high-income segments and an "access/security" message for emerging segments, rather than a generic brand blast.

Tools: Regression Analysis, T-Tests, Excel
04 — Statistical Simulation

The Law of Large Numbers

"Single data points can lie. It's only in the aggregate that the truth emerges."

Using Mark-Recapture simulation data, this model demonstrates the volatility of small sample sizes. Individual samples yielded population estimates ranging wildly from 170 to 500. However, the cumulative model stabilized at 305. This underscores the danger of reacting to single-data-point anomalies versus long-term trends.

Tools: Statistical Modeling, Excel
