Income-Wellbeing Correlation

"Higher income correlates with higher wellbeing, but the relationship isn't linear. After $100k, additional income yields diminishing returns."

Analysis of 1,000+ survey responses shows distinct consumer segments: low-income respondents ($28k-$42k) report wellbeing scores of 40-50, middle-income ($55k-$72k) score 62-70, and high-income ($95k-$150k) reach 78-88. The data reveals income thresholds where wellbeing improvements plateau, enabling more precise targeting of wellbeing-related products and services.
